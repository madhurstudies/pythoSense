Know exactly what your
plant is feeling

PythoSense reads soil temperature, humidity, and ambient light intensity from your plant using DHT11/DHT22 and LDR sensors — in real-time.

⚙️
DHT11 / DHT22 Sensors

The core of PythoSense relies on precision digital temperature and humidity sensors. The DHT series provides highly reliable, calibrated digital signal output.

  • 🌡️ Soil Temperature Maintains awareness of root comfort levels.
  • 💧 Humidity Tracking Ensures the microclimate around the plant is ideal.
💡
LDR Light Sensor

A Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) continuously monitors the ambient light intensity hitting the plant's leaves, crucial for photosynthesis.

  • ☀️ Photoperiod Tracking Detects daytime vs nighttime cycles.
  • 🔆 Intensity Measurement Warns if the plant is in overly shaded or harsh light.
📊
Advanced Diagnostics

Beyond raw sensor data, PythoSense calculates derived metrics using agricultural formulas to give you actionable insights.

  • 🌫️ Evaporation Index Calculated via Penman-Monteith (ET₀) approximations based on temp and humidity.
  • 🚿 Water Requirement Combines expected evaporation with current soil moisture to determine daily watering volume.
  • 🪱 Soil Moisture % Direct capacitive measurement of the soil's dielectric constant.
❤️
Plant Health Score Algorithm

All data streams are fed into an intelligent weighing algorithm that produces a unified 0-100 Health Score. This makes monitoring your plant's status as simple as reading a single grade. The scoring model evaluates individual parameters based on their immediate impact on plant vitality:

  • ⚖️ Weighted Distribution Moisture (40%), Light (25%), Temp (20%), and Humidity (15%) form the base metrics.
  • 🎯 Dynamic Deviation Metrics are compared against ideal thresholds. Deviations outside optimal bands reduce points based on a quadratic curve (stronger penalty for severe deviations).
  • 📉 Stress Multipliers If synergistic stress occurs (e.g., high heat + low moisture), an additional compound penalty multiplier (up to 1.5x) is applied.
  • 🛡️ Trailing Average A statistical smoothing function is applied over the trailing 15-minute window to eliminate fleeting anomalies and provide a stable health grade.

Mathematical Formulation:

BaseScore =
  100 - (
    (ΔMoisture² × 0.40) +
    (ΔLight × 0.25) +
    (ΔTemp² × 0.20) +
    (ΔHumidity × 0.15)
  )

Final Health Score = BaseScore × (1 - SynergisticStressMultiplier)

Where Δ represents the absolute percentage deviation from the plant's ideal target value. Variables like Temperature and Moisture are squared because extreme deviations in those core metrics are exponentially more dangerous to root systems than minor fluctuations.
